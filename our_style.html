Our Style

Agile all the way!

We strive to work in an agile devops environment where we are always moving.

When we agree to partner on a project whether it be new or maintenance, we always ensure we are in alignment with how we approach the integration service development, testing, deployment and delivery. At the time, we agree on a sprint cadence, stand-up times and participants and determine roles and responsibilities. This way everyone is clear from the get-go who is who in the zoo!

We have adopted AGILE DEVOPS as our delivery method. With this method, we focus on iterative, incremental, and evolutionary development. Develop small and release often. This way changes are released to consumers more frequently and sooner than in a traditional waterfall approach.

Our Vision, Value and Benefits

We believe, with our organization practicing good integration we can create an agile integrated digital ecosystem and gain:

  • Agility to keep critical business and financial information up-to-date and make better, faster business decisions
  • Visibility into the data flowing throughout your business so you can proactively manage processes and troubleshoot errors
  • Efficiency by connecting disparate applications and improving processes across the enterprise
  • Time and cost savings by eliminating manual processes and costly legacy systems, so IT resources can focus on strategic activities
  • Accelerated growth with the ability to bring on new business – and gain revenue – faster with out-of-the-box solutions that are easy to deploy

Future value/benefits:

  1. Allows us to integrate with partners or stakeholders
  2. Allow us to track and analyze a wide range of data (product/process improvements)
  3. Allows us decrease costs with better business or technology processes
  4. Allows us to break down silos between systems or business areas
  5. Modernizing ISB’s approach to service delivery

Our Integration Principles

  • All integration services are developed and delivered using agile methodologies
  • Be future friendly by determining re-usability (can this be developed in a way that can be reused by another application)
  • When integration components are developed, modern technology stacks will be used
  • Put clients first by ensuring clear integration requirements are documented and the solution fits those requirements
  • Design for flexibility and speed
  • Design with a digital ecosystem mindset
  • Ongoing engagement with vendor teams/organizations on new tech
  • Services deployments should be lightweight and easily manageable
  • Open standards and methodologies should be adopted in preference to proprietary solutions for integration
  • Mediation between systems (consumers and provider endpoints)
  • Service providers and consumers will use standard web service Application Programming Interfaces (APIs) to expose and obtain information from other services and follow the full API management lifecycle
  • When dealing with distributed systems integration should design interactions to be asynchronous to manage problems such as availability and latency
